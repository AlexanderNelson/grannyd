'Granny D'

American Political Activist | Doris "Granny D" Haddock

Her Life:

  • 1960- Campaigned against planned hydrogen bomb nuclear testing in Alaska, saving an Inuit fishing village.
  • 1972- Served on the Planning Board in Dublin, New Hampshire where she and her husband had retired.
  • 1999- at the age of 88, left the Rose Bowl Tournament of Roses Parade in Pasadena, California, to walk across the United States raising awareness of and attracting support for campaign finance reform.
  • 2000- Several dozen members of Congress walked the final miles with her during the final day's walk from Arlington National Cemetery to the Capitol in Washington D.C.
  • 2004- Became the Democratic candidate for a U.S. Senate seat in New Hampshire when the leading Democratic primary candidate left the race unexpectedly, because of a campaign-finance scandal. At 94, she was one of the oldest major-party candidates to ever run for the U.S. Senate.
  • 2007- HBO released a documentary, Run Granny Run, directed by Marlo Poras, about Granny D's 2004 Senate campaign.

Her Arrest:

    On April 21, 2000, Granny D, along with 31 others, was arrested for reading the Declaration of Independence in the Capitol and was charged with the offense of demonstrating in the Capitol Building. It was said to be a peaceable assembly, but the demonstrators were arrested by the Capitol Police.
"Your Honor, to the business at hand: the old woman who stands before you was arrested for reading the Declaration of Independence in America's Capitol Building. I did not raise my voice to do so and I blocked no hall. But if it is a crime to read the Declaration of Independence in our great hall, then I am guilty." -Granny D.
